Companies Buy Productivity, Not Software

Leaders are asked to do more with less effort. But most analytics programs still depend on manual interpretation: people pulling data, reconciling metrics, applying business rules, and writing narratives for executives.

Augmented Reasoning is a delivery system for custom AI agents that execute those repetitive decision workflows—consistently, with your policies, and with traceability—so teams spend less time interpreting and more time acting.

It is purpose-built for organizations that want decision intelligence: the capability to rapidly simulate, evaluate, and narrow complex operational and strategic scenarios—before committing deeper analytical resources.

Core outcomes:

  • Faster decisions from the same data
  • Less analyst burden on recurring explanations and reporting
  • Higher consistency in business-rule application
  • Better executive narratives delivered on a predictable cadence

Where it deploys:

  • Databricks (Agent endpoints, Lakehouse data, Unity Catalog governance)
What it integrates with:
  • Enterprise apps and portals (internal tools)
  • Dashboards and report engines (BI, scheduled reporting)

What Augmented Reasoning Agents Do

Automate the Decision Work Behind the Dashboard

Dashboards show metrics. Leaders still need explanations, prioritization, and recommended actions. Augmented Reasoning agents turn metrics into decisions by executing the same structured reasoning steps your best analysts and operators perform—at scale and on cadence.

Typical workflows include:

  • KPI variance attribution: “Where did it move, and what drove it?”
  • Business risk translation: “Does it matter, and who is impacted?”
  • Root-cause triage: “What broke: demand, supply, process, system?”
  • Next action: “What should we do next, and what are the tradeoffs?”
  • Narrative generation: “Write the executive summary and stakeholder brief.”
